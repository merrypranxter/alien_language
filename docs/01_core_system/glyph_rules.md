# Glyph Rules

To maintain cohesion, Oraphine glyphs follow several construction rules:

1. **One dominant anchor:** each glyph must have a main organizing element—either a stem, a chamber or a wedge.
2. **Limited parts:** most symbols contain one anchor, one body form and one to three modifiers or terminals.
3. **Repeated motifs:** eye chambers, split tails, top dots, offset crescents and broken halos appear across the inventory.
4. **Imperfect balance:** any symmetrical appearance is deliberately broken by offsetting a dot, tilting a fork or varying a crescent's size.
5. **Consistent weight:** lines are medium thickness; dots and wedges are filled; avoid excessive filigree or microscopic detail.

By adhering to these rules, newly designed glyphs will naturally belong to the Oraphine family.
