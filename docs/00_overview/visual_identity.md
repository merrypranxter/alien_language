# Visual Identity

Oraphine glyphs embody a **sacred‑tech** aesthetic: part ceremonial writing, part biotech interface. Key visual traits include:

- **Geometric bones and living curves:** every symbol combines straight stems or wedges with arcs and crescents.
- **Asymmetric balance:** forms are almost mirrored but deliberately offset or unbalanced.
- **Repeated motifs:** eye‑shaped chambers, forked terminals, triangular crowns, suspended dots, broken halos.
- **Medium weight strokes:** lines are neither too thin nor too thick; dots and filled wedges punctuate the script.
- **Tall proportions:** most glyphs fit into a slender rectangular footprint, with occasional circular sigils used sparingly.
