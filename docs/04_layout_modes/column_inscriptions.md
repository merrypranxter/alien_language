# Column Inscriptions

Column inscriptions are vertical stacks of glyphs reminiscent of temple pillars or monolithic panels. Guidelines:

- Stack common glyphs tightly with little horizontal variation.
- Begin or end the column with a sigil or elevated glyph to anchor it.
- Insert horizontal bars or small divider marks occasionally to break continuous stacks.
- Use vertical writing for monuments, ritual slabs and ceremonial banners.

Columns emphasize the tall proportions of Oraphine and evoke ancient liturgical texts.
