# Sigil Glyphs

Sigil glyphs are large, seal‑like symbols used as chapter starts, emblems, portal markings and relic seals. They often incorporate radial arrangements and nested shapes. Typical characteristics:

- A central chamber or node surrounded by crescents, wedges or rings.
- Symmetry that is slightly broken through offset accents.
- Multiple layers of crescents, halos or dots radiating outward.
- A larger footprint than normal characters, commanding attention.

Insert sigils at the beginning of inscriptions, around orbs, or as central elements in circular compositions.
