# Common Glyphs

Common glyphs are the everyday symbols of Oraphine. They are simple, quick to draw and form the bulk of longer inscriptions. Characteristics include:

- One stem or chamber as the anchor.
- One major modifier such as a crescent, wedge or fork.
- A single accent like a dot or bar.
- Narrow footprints that fit well in horizontal bands.

These glyphs are used repeatedly in text bands and columns and are occasionally modified with extra nodes or slashes to convey pseudo‑semantic nuance.
