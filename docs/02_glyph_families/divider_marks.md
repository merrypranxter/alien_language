# Divider Marks

Divider marks serve as punctuation and formatting in Oraphine. They break strings of glyphs into phrases, sections or lists. Examples include:

- A single solid node indicating a minor pause.
- A pair of vertical nodes marking a phrase break.
- A triangular cluster of three nodes for section emphasis.
- A broken ring signifying a sacred pause or title break.
- A short double slash used for warnings or corrupted lines.
- A mini circular sigil marking the end of a stanza.

Place divider marks between groups of glyphs to create rhythm and hierarchical structure.
