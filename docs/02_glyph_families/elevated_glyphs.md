# Elevated Glyphs

Elevated glyphs are special symbols used for names, titles, sacred words, commands and warnings. They are more complex than common glyphs and stand out in compositions. Features include:

- Stacked or doubled structures, such as a chamber crowned by wedges.
- Additional floating marks like halos, double nodes or mini crescents.
- Enclosed shapes combined with crowns, forks or bars.
- Taller or wider proportions that break the rhythm of common text.

Use elevated glyphs sparingly to mark significance within a string of common glyphs.
