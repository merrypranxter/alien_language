# Pseudo-Grammar

Although Oraphine is primarily a visual language, it benefits from an underlying pseudo‑grammar that makes strings of glyphs feel intentional. Suggested behaviour:

- **Chambers** often act like nouns or identities, representing beings, objects or vessels.
- **Forks** convey action, force or emission, giving verbs or dynamic qualities.
- **Wedges** indicate direction, danger or command, acting as imperatives or markers.
- **Crescents** modulate energy, psychic quality or celestial association, serving as adjectives or adverbs.
- **Nodes** convey plurality, emphasis or intensity.
- **Slashes/Bars** denote negation, corruption or inversion.
- **Halos** mark sacredness, titles or cosmic affiliation.

Clusters of glyphs can combine these parts to create rough semantics—for example, a chamber followed by a fork indicates a person acting, while a wedge plus a slash implies a prohibited state.
